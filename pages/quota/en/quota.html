What is Quota?

Quota is used to measure computing resources in the Vite system. The unit of quota is Unit Transaction, or UT for short.

In the Wallet, there are three Quota-related terms:

Quota Limit: Maximum quota a user can get for a certain period of time.

Spendable Quota: Quota that can be used to send transactions at the moment

Quota(of a Transaction): How huch quota is needed to send this transaction

Every sent transaction causes spendable quota to decrease, spendable quota cost will recover after a period of time.

How to Get Quota?

Staking（You may withdraw staking for free 3 days later）

You get quota by staking VITE. The more you stake, the more quota you get.

Click to check staking amount needed in different transaction scenarios

PoW Compution（（No staking required; Vite pays for the computation cost）

PoW is short for Power of Work; When your quota from staking is insufficient for a transaction, Vite will spend its resources to compute PoW and get you the quota necessary for this transaction.

What is Dynamic Quota?

When too many transactions simultaneously take place, Dynamic Quota mode will kick in. In this mode, the quota amount required for a transaction won’t change; however, your Quota Limit will be temporarily reduced; also, during this time, you won’t be able to get quota from PoW. When network traffic clears up, your Quota Limit will go back to its original level.
